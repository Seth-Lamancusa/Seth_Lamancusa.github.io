Seth Lamancusa (Born October 7, 2002) is a Data Analytics major attending The Ohio State University pursuing a Cognitive Science minor. Interests include Artificial Intelligence (AI), philosophy, art, and the Oxford comma.

This page is simultaneously a personal catalogue and a professional development tool. I want a platform on which to showcase my projects, accomplishments, and experience. I aim to use this as a living outlet for my own passions, interests, and personal growth. I didn't want just another Bootstrap template, and I wanted to get some experience with HTML and CSS, so I landed on making a Wikipedia clone. Unfortunately, Wikipedia overhauled their frontend for the first time in a decade just months after I made it. C'est la vie.

I grew up in the suburbs of Northeast Ohio. I always had an affinity for math and coding. Following a brief but enthusiastic gamedev phase during junior high I leaned into marching band and drum corps, occupying most of my time and energy during high school. During my Junior and Senior years I discovered a fascination with AI. It appealed to both my technical and philosophical faculties, and has become something of an obsession since then.

During college, I've discovered the rich and encompassing field of Cognitive Science (of which AI is a component) and come a long way toward understanding the state of the various constituent academic fields and of the technological space. I'm currently working as a Research Assistant in Dr. Julie Golomb's Vision and Cognitive Neuroscience lab and am learning as much as I can about AI on my own time. I'm excited to continue to narrow down my area of interest and get some tangible experience with AI systems.

Education

High School

I attended Stow-Munroe Falls High School for four years. Involved with Speech and Debate, Marching Band, Concert Band, and co-founding our very own Esports team, I did my best to remain active in our community while leaning into those extracurriculars which inspired a unique passion.

As far as school work was concerned, I challenged myself to maintain a high level of academic achievement. I took a total of 8 college-level AP courses thoughout my time in high school, none of which resulted in test scores lower than 4. I engaged especially with AP English Literature and Composition and AP English Language and Composition, but skipped AP Psychology to get ahead on college GE's with AP Micro and Macroeconomics. In a twist of fate, I would wind up pursuing a Cognitive Science minor after graduating high school, and neither of those two economics classes would be eligible for transfer credit.

Experience

Dunkin': Crew Member, August 2019 - February 2021

I woked part time at Dunkin for several years in high school. It was fast-paced work and as a crew member, I was responsible for working the register, drive-thru, drink and sandwich stations, and completing the cleaning checklist.

The Ohio State University: Software 1 Grader, January 2022 - May 2022

During the spring semester of my Freshman year, I worked part time as a grader for Software 1, which I had taken the semester prior. I was offered the role for my exemplary performance in the course and working the position was highly beneficial for my own understanding of the course concepts: namely the basics of Java, and implementations of various common data structures and algorithms.

The Ohio State University: Research Assistant, June 2023 - present

Over the summer and throughout this semester, I've worked part time in Dr. Julie Golomb's Vision and Cognitive Neuroscience lab. I'm responsible for running subjects for my mentor's experiment, attending lab meetings, and reading relevant literature. I frequently speak with my mentor regarding her experimental design and data processing techniques. I've also been exposed to a lot of MATLAB and statistical methodology.

The reason I applied for and took this position was for its adjacency to cognitive science. Since taking it, I've realized that my interests lie more in the realm of CS, AI, and philosophy than they do in behavioral psychology, but I don't regret a minute I've spent with Dr. Golomb and her team. It has been a really enriching experience to watch how research is done up close and get hands-on with the methodology and with the data.

Percussion

Me playing in Matrix A final run

The hobby, and singular pursuit to which I have undoubtedly dedicated the most time is that of percussion. I picked up a pair of sticks for the first time in 5th grade and participated in my middle schools' concert band programs throughout 8th grade. The advent of marching band in high school and the mentorship of an older student who was involved with independent drum corps and indoor programs (thanks Alex) accelerated the growth of my passion for the sport of marching percussion, while experimenting with increasingly sophisticated keyboard percussion techniques inspired a real love for the musicality of concert and solo percussion.

Fulfilling the roles of squad leader and section leader at my high school for multiple years and participating in WGI's Matrix A 2021 indoor group, then going on to prepare to tryout for The Best Damn Band In The Land (TBDBITL) two years running, participating in the university's athletic band my Freshman year and practicing thousands of hours besides, it's safe to say that among the projects and skills I've developed, and among the passions I've cultivated, percussion outranks the rest.

Despite this, at the end of the summer before my Sophomore year, a week before tryouts and after having worked all summer to prepare, I decided against trying out. The words of Paul Morphy echoed in my mind: "The ability to play chess is the sign of a gentleman. The ability to play chess well is the sign of a wasted life.". My interest in AI had become undeniable and I knew just how time consuming being a member of TBDBITL is. If I were to have continued with marching band, I would have had hundreds of hours fewer to dedicate to developing my technical skillset. So I hung up my garment bag for the last time and haven't regretted it once.

Software

It all started with block coding in my seventh grade STEM class. I liked Scratch, and I liked the idea of using gamedev as a medium through which to realize my ideas, so in eighth grade when presented with the opportunity to pursue whatever type of project I desired as part of a once-weekly "genius hour" segment of my language arts class, I chose to make a game using Scratch. After that, I experimented with Unity for awhile before my focus was more or less redirected to drumline, and my interest in gamedev transformed into a more holistic interest in software, which I developed through participation in a career program at my high school, through which I learned the basics of Java, SQL, and HTML/CSS/JS.

I dabbled with coding throughout high school but it's only through formal education and abandoning drumming that I've become really comfortable with languages like Python, Java, and JavaScript. There are a number of projects on my GitHub which reflect my interest in software better than I could convey in words here. I look forward to continuing to learn to effectively use software as a tool to realize my ideas.

Reading

Over the past couple of years, I've rediscovered a love of books that lay dormant for a long time. I'm much more interested in nonfiction than I was in gradeschool, and my reading list is longer than ever. Among my favorite authors today are Carl Sagan and Sean Carroll, and I've come to realize the power of literature in a way that never stuck with me when I was young.
